About Me

I'm a licensed architect and product manager who bridges the worlds of design, technology, and storytelling. Currently at Higharc, I lead product development focused on systematizing and automating residential design and documentation processes—bringing computational thinking to one of our most fundamental human needs: shelter. My architectural journey has taken me from intimate affordable housing projects to massive 1-million-square-foot hospital complexes during my time at renowned firms like KieranTimberlake and Ballinger, where I learned that great design scales from the deeply personal to the monumentally complex.

Beyond traditional practice, I'm drawn to understanding how people create spaces and communities in challenging circumstances. This curiosity led me to South America, where I directed and filmed a 45-minute documentary exploring informal settlements—a project that was screened internationally and deepened my appreciation for resourcefulness and human resilience. Originally from North Carolina, I bring together my technical expertise in digital design tools, hands-on project leadership experience, and a filmmaker's eye for narrative to help shape the future of how we design and build our built environment.
